Product management is the practice of deciding which problems deserve investment, for whom and with what expected outcome. It connects strategy, discovery, design, technology, business and operations to learn whether a product creates value.

The Product Manager is not the boss of every function or the sole owner of ideas. The role creates clarity about the problem and context, makes decisions testable and coordinates the learning a team needs.

## Strategy and discovery

Strategy turns business objectives and user needs into choices about audience, problem, value, advantage and constraints. Discovery reduces uncertainty about value, usability, feasibility and risk through research, behavioral data, prototypes and experiments.

## Prioritization, roadmap and delivery

Prioritization compares expected impact, evidence, cost, risk and dependencies. A roadmap communicates problems, outcomes and sequencing. Delivery includes acceptance, observability, accessibility and rollback; product work continues after launch through adoption, quality, support, cost and unintended effects.

## Metrics and AI

Connect behavior, experience quality and business or user outcomes. More artifacts are activity, not proof of value. AI can support synthesis, exploration and execution, but adds uncertainty, variable cost and new risks. Define evaluation, oversight and failure behavior during discovery.
